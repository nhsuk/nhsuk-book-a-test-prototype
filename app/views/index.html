You can get a test if you:

  • have coronavirus symptoms now (a high temperature or a new, continuous cough)
  • live with someone who has coronavirus symptoms

If you need medical advice about your symptoms, use the 111 coronavirus service.

Type of test

You can ask for a test to check if you have coronavirus now (antigen test). This test usually involves taking a swab of the inside of your nose and the back of your throat, using a long cotton bud. You can do the swab yourself or someone can do it for you.

There is another kind of test (antibody test) that checks to see if you have had the virus. It is done after you get better. This test is not available yet.

Who can get the test

People in hospital, NHS staff and key workers are getting priority. You can find a list of key workers being tested on GOV.UK.

If you or someone you live with is self-isolating at home with coronavirus symptoms, you can ask for a test. There is no guarantee that you will get a test. It depends on how many tests are available each day in different parts of the country.

If you are asking for someone else and the person is aged 13 and over, check that they are happy for you to ask for a test for them.

You need to get the test done in the first 5 days of having symptoms. It's best to ask for the test in the first 3 days as it may take a day or two to arrange.

Information:

Please help the NHS and only ask for a test if you or someone you live with has symptoms now.
